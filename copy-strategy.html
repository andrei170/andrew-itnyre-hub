Foundation

Copy Strategy - Andrew Itnyre (@mastersroofing.memphis)

Foundation deliverable · Voice, brand pillars, headline bank, copy framing AG1 will write to.

Voice Profile

Who Andrew is on the page: 2nd-gen roofer who grew up in the trade, runs sales and marketing at Masters Roofing Memphis, and is now building a brand that extends beyond the Memphis market. Direct. Trade-grounded. Not a course-seller, not a guru. A real operator who does the work and has the calluses to prove it.

Register: Conversational, direct, unpretentious. Memphis working-class trade roots. The opposite of polished corporate speak. Think "how I'd explain this to someone on the job site" not "how I'd write it for LinkedIn."

What Andrew never sounds like: - Hype-driven ("crushing it," "game-changer," "skyrocketing") - Vague and inspirational ("unlock your potential," "live your best life") - Corporate soft ("leverage synergies," "holistic approach") - Fake humble ("I'm just a roofer but...")

What Andrew always sounds like: - Specific ("we raised our average ticket 22% when we stopped apologizing") - Trade-grounded ("three things I check before I quote a roof") - Straight ("if you run a family trade business, this is for you. If you don't, it's not") - Earned authority ("I grew up on roofs. This is what I actually know")

Brand Pillars

Pillar 1 - The Trade is Real

Masters Roofing Memphis is a real local business, not a backdrop for content. Andrew installs roofs, manages crews, handles sales. Everything he teaches comes from doing, not from studying business books.

Copy applications: Lead with the work. Proof of trade competence before any teaching or positioning.

Pillar 2 - The 2nd-Gen Handoff

Taking over a family trade business is a specific challenge no one talks about. The dynamics, the authority, the expectations, the growth tension between the founder generation and the next. Andrew owns this lane.

Copy applications: The 2nd-gen story is a hook, a differentiator, and a positioning claim. Use it in headlines, opening lines, and course framing.

Pillar 3 - Build the Brand, Not Just the Business

Andrew is building @mastersroofing.memphis as a national brand while running a local business. Brand partnerships, content momentum, operator audience. The trade can be a media brand.

Copy applications: Speaks to the ambition of the audience - other operators who want the brand partnership and the national audience, not just the local reputation.

Framing Rules for All Copy

  1. Lead with specificity, not aspiration. "22% average ticket increase" beats "boost your revenue." "25K operators watching" beats "growing audience."

  2. The 2nd-gen angle is a first-mover position. Every piece of copy should imply: Andrew is doing something no one else is doing in this space, not just doing it better than others.

  3. Trade first, course second. The framing is always "here is what we do at Masters Roofing Memphis" before "here is what you can learn from it." Practitioner credibility, then teaching frame.

  4. No minute CTAs. No "book a 20-minute call" in first-touch copy. Hub URL does the work. Call invitation lives in the hub itself.

  5. No payment terms in outreach. Wire, Whop, price points - none of this in cold DMs, emails, or LinkedIn first touches.

  6. First-mover framing, not catch-up framing. Andrew is building something new, not trying to become Hunter Ballew. The copy reflects that. "First to own this lane" not "here is your chance to do what established operators do."

Sign-Off Standards

  • Short: "- Andrei (AG1)"
  • Long: "- Andrei / AG1 Consulting Ltd"
  • Never: em dashes before the sign-off

Quality Checklist
