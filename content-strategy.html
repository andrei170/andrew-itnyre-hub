Core Content Positioning

Andrew's content lane: 2nd-gen roofer showing other family trade operators and young contractors how to run a real roofing business - sales, brand, team, and the family dynamics no one talks about on camera.

What makes Andrew different from every other roofing content creator: - Real trade, not theory - he runs the family business, he does not teach from a course-creator background - 2nd-gen angle is genuinely underrepresented - the dynamics of taking over a family trade are worth a content category on their own - Memphis market specificity - local trade credibility, not generic contractor advice - Brand partnerships signal commercial maturity - other young operators want to know how to get there

IG Short-Form Content Roadmap

Pillar 1 - Trade Education (40%)

Project showcases + process content + "how we do it" reels. Proof of work, not just talk.

Format: 30-60 second reel, voiceover or on-camera. Show the work. Explain one specific thing per reel.

Examples: - Roof install process start to finish (time-lapse + narration) - "How we handle a difficult insurance claim in Memphis" - "What a good quote process looks like vs a bad one"

Pillar 2 - Business and Operator Insights (35%)

The content the operator audience came for. Family business dynamics, sales, team management, brand building.

Format: Talking head or text-on-screen. One insight per reel. Direct and specific.

Examples: - Hook 5 (near-loss story) - Hook 12 (how brand deals came to them) - Hook 19 (family members at work)

Pillar 3 - Personal Brand and Credibility (25%)

Andrew as a person - young contractor, 2nd-gen story, Memphis roots, what the day actually looks like.

Format: B-roll + narration, or raw talking head. Authenticity over production value.

Examples: - A day on a job site - "What my dad told me when I took over sales" - Before and after of a Memphis roofing job with story behind the homeowner

Content Calendar Structure (Weekly)

Day Content Type Pillar
Monday Trade education reel Pillar 1
Wednesday Business insight reel Pillar 2
Friday Personal brand / story Pillar 3
Saturday Project showcase photo or carousel Pillar 1

3-4 posts per week. Consistency over volume at this stage.

Brand Partnership Content Angle

When posting brand partnership content, keep it in the trade voice: "This is what we actually use on jobs. Here is why." Never feel like a sponsor read. The audience can smell it.

Format for brand posts: 1 reel showing the product in real use on a job site. Narration explains the specific reason you use it, not the brand's talking points.

Ratio: Maximum 1 in 5 posts is brand-adjacent. Keep the feed 80% genuine trade content.

Lead Magnet Content Funnel

Once the Family Roofing Operator Scorecard is live:

  • Bio link routes to the scorecard as the primary CTA
  • 1 reel per month specifically drives to the scorecard ("take this if you want to know where your roofing operation actually stands")
  • Every business-insight reel ends with a soft mention: "link in bio if you want the full breakdown"

Notes

  • Do not use hashtag spam. Memphis-specific + trade-specific hashtags only.
  • Reels outperform static at 25K. Prioritize video.
  • Authenticity is the brand. Do not produce content that feels polished beyond what Andrew would naturally produce. The rawness is a feature.
  • The 2nd-gen angle is untapped in roofing content. Own it before anyone else does.
